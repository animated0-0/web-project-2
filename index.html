Treaty 5

Where is the Treaty 5 located?

Treaty 5 is located in parts of Manitoba, Saskatchewan, and Ontario, including areas around Lake Winnipeg, the Saskatchewan River, and extending north to the 60th parallel.

Where is Treaty 5 negotiated?

Treaty 5 was negotiated in 1875 to 76 between the Canadian government and the Ojibwe and Swampy Cree peoples around Lake Winnipeg.

What communities are involved and what their ethnic affiliation is or what Nation they are from?

Includes First Nations primarily from the Cree and Ojibwe nations. These communities are located in Manitoba, Saskatchewan, and Ontario.

What are the historic terms of the Treaty, meaning what was included in the Treaty?

  • Land Surrender and Reserves:

    Families receive 160 acres of land per family of five.

  • Treaty annuities and benefits:

    Treaty annuities and benefits are the payments and goods provided to Indigenous peoples in return for land.

  • Control of Liquor:

    The government promised to regulate the liquor trade within Indigenous territories to prevent alcohol-related issues.

What makes Treaty 5 different from other Treaties in Canada?

Treaty 5, signed in 1875, is distinct from other Canadian treaties due to its individualized negotiation approach, smaller reserve allocations, lower financial provisions, inclusion of water rights, and delayed boundary clarifications.

How is the Treaty being implemented today?

Treaty 5 is realized in the form of such urban reserves as Kapyong in Winnipeg and the Long Plain First Nation gas station that create jobs and economic opportunity. Such projects and land entitlement agreements promote growth and development of the communities.
